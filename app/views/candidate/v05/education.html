Education

To be eligible for teacher training, you need a:

  • UK degree (or international equivalent)
  • GCSE in Maths, grade 4 (previously grade C)
  • GCSE in English, grade 4 (previously grade C)

To teach at primary school, you also need a minimum grade 4 GCSE in science. You can learn more about eligibility at Get into Teaching.

I don’t have GCSEs in maths and English

You can also be accepted onto a course if you have older qualifications like O levels or international qualifications equivalent to GCSEs.

If you don’t have the right GCSE or its equivalent yet, you can still apply. Your training provider can help you get the right qualification in time for your course.

I studied for my qualifications outside the UK

If you studied for your degree or GCSE equivalents overseas, have you checked your qualifications are recognised in the UK?

If not, please visit UK NARIC for help validating your qualifications. You’ll need a statement of comparability to show to your training provider.

I haven't completed my degree

If you are still studying, give us your predicted grade and date of graduation. If there's another reason why you didn't complete your degree, your training provider may be able to help.
